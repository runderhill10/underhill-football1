📖 About This Playbook

🎯 Philosophy

Every play, drill, and practice is designed to be FUN FIRST. At this age, kids learn through play. We teach basics simply, celebrate effort, and build love for the game.

👧👦 Ages 5–8

Short attention spans = short sessions! Drills last 5–8 minutes max. We use colors, animals, and fun names so kids remember plays easily.

🏆 Team Size

Designed for 5-on-5 flag football — the most common youth format. Easily adaptable to 4-on-4 or 7-on-7 formats.

📐 Field Setup

30 yard field length, 20 yards wide. Two 15-yard halves. 5-yard end zones. 4 downs to reach midfield, 4 more to score. No rushing the QB.

🌟 Key Positions (Simplified)

🌟 QB (Quarterback)

Calls the play, takes the snap, throws or hands off. The team leader. Use your most confident kid — rotate often so everyone gets a chance!

🚀 Center

Snaps the ball between their legs to start every play. Simple job — but very important. Great role for a focused, reliable player.

⚡ Receivers (3)

Run routes and catch passes. Teach: look for the ball, run straight, then turn around! Split wide left, right, and middle (slot).

🏃 Runner / RB

Takes handoffs from QB and runs. Teach: hold the ball tight, run toward open space, protect your flags!

Official Rules

Flag Football By-Laws — Underhill Agency

📋
👟 Equipment
1
League ProvidesThe Marshall Recreation Department provides jerseys, flags, belts, and footballs. You just need to show up!
2
Shoes RequiredPlayers must wear shoes. Football cleats are encouraged — but no exposed metal allowed.
3
No JewelryAll watches and jewelry must be removed before play. Safety first!
4
Jersey RulesJerseys must be worn during play. If the jersey hangs below the belt line, it must be tucked in so flags are visible.
5
Clothing & ProtectionSweatpants or shorts are recommended. Mouthguards are encouraged for added safety.
🏈 Game Rules
1
First PossessionThe team listed first on the schedule starts with the ball. After halftime, the other team gets possession to start the second half.
2
3 Plays to Midfield, 3 to ScoreEach possession starts at your own 5-yard line. You get 3 plays to cross midfield. Once past midfield, you get 3 more plays to score a touchdown.
3
Turnover on DownsIf the offense fails to cross midfield OR fails to score, possession changes. The new offense always starts at their own 5-yard line.
4
All Turnovers → 5-Yard LineAll possession changes — except interceptions — begin at the new offense's 5-yard line.
5
Halftime SwitchTeams switch sides at halftime. The team that started on defense gets the ball to begin the second half.
🏟️ Field Rules
1
⚠️ No-Run ZonesThere are No-Run Zones located 5 yards before midfield AND 5 yards before the end zone. No running plays allowed from inside these zones — passing only!
2
Out of BoundsStepping ON the boundary line counts as out of bounds. Receivers need at least one foot clearly inbounds to complete a catch.
⏱️ Timing

⏰ Game Length

Two 18-minute halves. The clock only stops for timeouts or if the official calls it (e.g., injury).

🕐 Timeouts

Each team gets one 60-second timeout per game. Use it wisely!

🍊 Halftime

Halftime lasts three minutes. Quick water break, swap sides, and go!

📢 Snap Clock

Once the ball is placed, the offense has 20 seconds to snap the ball. Huddle fast!

🟢 Live Ball / Dead Ball
1
Ball is Live at the SnapThe ball is live as soon as it is snapped and stays live until the official blows the whistle.
2
Catches In BoundsA catch is good if the player controls the ball with at least one foot down inbounds.
3
No Mimicking SignalsThe defense may not copy or mimic offensive signals. That's a penalty!
4
SubstitutionsSubstitutions are allowed on any dead ball. Swap players freely between plays.
🔴 When is the Play Dead?

🏈 Ball Hits Ground

No fumbles in flag football! If the ball hits the ground — from a drop, bad snap, or any reason — the play is immediately dead.

🚩 Flag is Pulled

Play ends when the ball carrier's flag is pulled. Also dead if the carrier has one flag or NO flags while holding the ball.

📡 Receiver with No Flags

If a receiver catches the ball but has one or no flags attached, the play is ruled dead immediately at the spot of the catch.

📏 Out of Bounds

Play is dead the moment the ball carrier steps on or outside the boundary line.

7️⃣ Pass Clock Expires

The QB has 7 seconds to throw after the snap. If the clock expires, the play is dead and the ball goes back to the original line of scrimmage.

📣 Inadvertent Whistle

If the official accidentally blows the whistle, the offense can either take the ball at the spot of the whistle (lose the down) OR replay the down from the original line.

🏃 Running, Passing & Receiving
1
Where is the Ball Spotted?The ball is spotted where the runner's FEET are when the flag is pulled — not where the ball is.
2
QB Can't Cross the LineThe quarterback cannot run past the line of scrimmage. However, the QB may scramble behind the line to avoid a sack.
3
No Laterals or PitchesNo lateral passes or pitches allowed. The player receiving a handoff MAY pass the ball from behind the line of scrimmage.
4
Rush After HandoffOnce the ball is handed off, ALL defenders become eligible to rush immediately — no 7-yard restriction applies after a handoff.
5
No Diving or JumpingBall carriers may NOT dive, leap, or jump to avoid flag pulls. Players may only leave their feet to avoid a collision.
6
No BlockingNo blocking, screening, or running alongside the ball carrier is allowed at any time.
7
7-Second Pass ClockAll passes must be thrown from behind the line of scrimmage. The QB has 7 seconds to release the ball or the play is dead.
8
Everyone is EligibleAll players — including the QB (if a handoff happened first) — are eligible to receive passes.
9
One Player in MotionOnly one offensive player may be in motion at a time before the snap.
10
Simultaneous PossessionIf both a receiver and defender catch the ball at the same time, the offense gets possession.
11
InterceptionsInterceptions can be returned — EXCEPT on conversions (extra point attempts) after a touchdown.
🚩 Rushing the Passer & Flag Pulling
1
7-Yard Rush LineAll rushers must start at least 7 yards from the line of scrimmage before the snap.
2
Wait for the HandoffDefenders must wait until the ball is actually handed off before they can rush. No early rushing the QB!
3
Can't Rush QB on Pass PlaysRushers may NOT charge the QB unless a handoff has occurred. After a handoff, rushing is allowed from anywhere.
4
No Contact on the PasserRushers may not make contact with the QB. Offense may not block or impede any rusher either.
5
Sacks & SafetiesA sack occurs if the QB's flag is pulled behind the line of scrimmage. If this happens in the end zone, the defense scores a Safety (2 points).
6
No Tackling or StrippingAbsolutely no tackling or attempting to strip the ball from a carrier. Flags only!
7
Flag Falls Off = PulledIf a ball carrier's flag falls off on its own (without being pulled), it is treated the same as a flag pull — the play is dead.
8
No Flag Guarding or Stiff ArmsBall carriers cannot guard their flags with their hands/arms or use a stiff arm. Penalty will be called!
⚡ Quick Reference — Key Numbers

🔢 Possession Numbers

3 plays to cross midfield
3 plays to score after midfield
5-yard line — where every drive starts

⏰ Time Numbers

18 min per half
3 min halftime
60 sec timeout (1 per team)
20 sec to snap the ball
7 sec pass clock

📏 Distance Numbers

7 yards — rushers must start back
5 yards — No-Run Zones before midfield and end zone

🚫 Always Illegal

Blocking • Tackling • Diving • Laterals • Stiff arms • Flag guarding • Exposed metal cleats • Jewelry

Underhill Agency Playbook

Offensive Plays

Simple routes — big fun! Run the ball, throw the ball, score!

⚡
🏃 Running Plays
🐰 Bunny Run (Sweep Right)
Running
QB takes snap, turns and hands off to RB who runs to the right sideline. Wide receiver on right side goes straight to clear space. Best play to start with!
🐢 Turtle Run (Sweep Left)
Running
Mirror of Bunny Run — RB lined up left and sweeps left. Great for changing direction and keeping defense guessing. Kids love the animal names!
🐂 Bull Rush (Up the Middle)
Running
QB hands off to RB who runs straight up the middle. Short yardage play — great on 3rd or 4th down when you need just a few yards to the first down marker.
🦊 Fox Scramble (QB Run)
Running
Receivers run wide to spread defense. QB keeps the ball and runs for open space. Works great when the QB is fast and confident. Kids go wild for this one!
📡 Passing Plays
🚀 Rocket (Go Routes)
Passing
Both outside receivers run straight down the field as fast as they can (Go route). Slot receiver runs a curl for a safe dump-off. QB picks whoever is open. Simple and fun!
🎣 Hook & Hope (Out Routes)
Passing
Outside receivers run 5 steps forward, stop, turn toward QB. Deep slot receiver goes long. QB throws quickly to the hitch. Teach kids: 5 steps → STOP → turn and look!
✂️ Scissors (Crossing Routes)
Passing
Both outside receivers run 5 yards forward then cross the field toward each other. Creates natural picks and open receivers. Slot goes deep as decoy. Look for the crossing receiver!
⭐ Star Power (TD Play)
Big Play
The touchdown special! Left WR runs corner route (diagonal to end zone corner). Right WR runs post (angled to center). Slot runs short flat as safety. QB throws to the corner — it's a TD!
Underhill Agency Playbook

Defensive Plays

Pull that flag! Protect the end zone!

🛡️
🛡️ Defensive Formations
🏰 Castle Defense (Zone)
Defense
Each defender covers an area (zone) of the field, not a specific player. D4 & D5 rush after 5 seconds. Easiest defense to teach beginners — "stay in your castle!"
🕵️ Shadow Defense (Man-to-Man)
Defense
Each defender follows one specific offensive player everywhere. "Be their shadow!" D5 is the rover/spy who can rush QB. Teach kids to watch the player, not the ball, until thrown.
⚡ Blitz Rush
Defense
Two defenders (rushers) come at QB after snap. Three defenders stay back in zone coverage. Best used on passing downs! Only for older/more experienced kids in this age group.
🎯 Defense Teaching Keys

👀 Eyes Up

Watch the QB's eyes and the ball. When the QB looks at a receiver, that's where the pass is going. React to the ball!

🤲 Flag Pulling

Reach for the flag with both hands. Pull DOWN, not sideways. Practice on a dummy belt before live play. Don't grab the player!

🏃 Get in Position

Stay between your receiver and the end zone. Don't let them get behind you! Teach "cushion" — stay 3-4 yards back to start.

📣 Communicate

Call out "I got left!" or "I got right!" so everyone knows their job. Talk on every play. Defense wins with communication!

Underhill Agency Practice

Practice Drills

Fun drills that teach real skills — every session!

🏃
🤸 Warm-Up Drills (Always First!)

🏃 Freeze Tag Football

5 min • All ages
Coach is "it" — kids run around trying not to get tagged. When tagged, freeze with feet apart until teammate crawls under legs. Gets kids moving and laughing immediately!

🐸 Frog Jumps

3 min • All ages
Kids line up, squat like frogs, then jump forward 5 times. Race format! Great for leg strength and explosiveness. "Ready, Set, RIBBIT!" 🐸

🐍 Snake Run

3 min • All ages
Kids line up in a snake formation and weave between cones together. Last kid in line runs to front. Teaches direction changes while staying together as a team.

🏈 Throwing & Catching Drills

🎯 Target Practice

8 min
Hang a hula hoop from a pole at kid height. QB throws through the hoop from 5 yards. Move back 2 yards each success. Use soft foam footballs for beginners!

👐 Catch & Go

8 min
QB throws 5 yards to receiver. Receiver catches, tucks ball, runs 5 more yards. Teaches the "catch, tuck, run" sequence. No stopping after the catch!

🦅 Eagle Eyes

6 min
Coach holds up colored cards as ball is thrown. Receiver must shout the color WHILE catching. Forces kids to watch the ball with focus. Makes catching a habit!

🏀 Wall Ball

5 min
Pairs stand 5 yards apart. One throws gently, other catches with only hands (no body trapping). Switch every 10 catches. Develops soft hands early.

🚩 Flag Pulling Drills

🎀 Flag Belt Relay

8 min
Two teams. Ball carrier runs between 4 cones in a zigzag. Defenders try to pull flag before carrier reaches the end. Trains evasion AND flag pulling at the same time!

🦁 Lion & Gazelle

8 min
3 "lions" (defenders) vs 5 "gazelles" (runners). Gazelles try to cross the field. Lions pull flags to stop them. Count how many gazelles make it across. Kids love this game!

🎯 One-on-One Open Field

10 min
Ball carrier starts at cone, defender 5 yards away. Go on "HUT!" Teach kids to run at angles, not straight. Defender must angle-chase. Great for advanced players.

🏃 Route Running Drills

✈️ Airplane Routes

10 min
Lay colored cones: Red = stop and turn (hitch), Blue = run straight (go), Yellow = run diagonal (corner). Call out a color and kids run that route. Makes routes visual and memorable!

📦 Box Route

8 min
Set up a 5-yard square with cones. Receiver runs each side in order. Teaches sharp cuts and directional changes. Time each kid and make it a race! Fastest wins a high-five from coach.

🎵 Freeze Route

6 min
Kids run routes while music plays. When music stops, freeze and show a throwing target with hands up. Fun and teaches body position. Use their favorite song!

🧠 Team Scrimmage Drills

⚡ 7-on-7 Walk-Through

10 min
Run plays at WALKING SPEED. No flags, no rushing. Just learning where to line up and what route to run. Slow practice = fast games. Do this BEFORE every live scrimmage.

🎲 Red Zone Challenges

12 min
Start offense at the 10-yard line. Only 2 plays to score a touchdown. Swap offense/defense after each series. Great for end-of-practice game scenarios. Very high energy!

Underhill Agency Coaching Guide

Coach's Tip Sheet

What the best youth coaches know!

💡
🧠 Coaching Philosophy
😂

Fun > Winning (Always)

At ages 5–8, if kids are laughing and smiling, you're winning. Skill development is secondary to making them love the sport. A kid who loves football at 7 will be good at 12.

⏱️

Short Drills, High Energy

5–8 year olds have 5–8 minutes of focused attention per activity. Keep every drill under 10 minutes. The moment they're bored, move on. Better to end a drill early than 2 minutes late.

🎉

Celebrate Everything

Good throw, bad throw, good effort — celebrate it all. Use a "Great Job!" signal (fist pump, high-five, thumbs up) liberally. Positive reinforcement builds confidence 10x faster than correction.

🔄

Rotate Positions Every Week

Every kid should play every position across the season. This builds complete football IQ, keeps things fair, and prevents "star player" burnout at age 7. You'll find surprising natural talent!

🐌

Slow = Fast in Practice

Walk-throughs at full speed make game speed automatic. The temptation is to scrimmage immediately — resist it. 10 minutes of slow walk-through = 30 minutes of confused live practice.

🗣️

Simple Language Only

No "shift your hips", "read the coverage", or "release off the line." Say "run straight then STOP!" or "when you hear HUT, go fast!" Animal names for plays help enormously.

🤲

Parent Engagement

Send parents a weekly "homework" tip — just one thing to practice at home (e.g., "10 gentle catches in the yard"). Parent involvement doubles skill development between practices.

🏅

End Every Practice Positively

Last 3 minutes: gather in a circle, coach says one specific positive about each player ("I loved how Alex caught that tough one!"), then team cheer. They'll be excited for next practice!

📊 Quick Reference — Game Day Calls

🏃 Need a Run?

  • Short yardage → Bull Rush
  • Outside space → Bunny or Turtle
  • Fast QB → Fox Scramble

📡 Need a Pass?

  • Open field → Rocket (Go routes)
  • Quick ball → Hook & Hope
  • Beat defense → Scissors
  • Need TD → Star Power

🛡️ Defense Call?

  • Default → Castle (Zone)
  • Tight coverage → Shadow (Man)
  • Stop QB → Blitz Rush

🆘 Team is Struggling?

  • Call timeout for water + reset
  • Pick ONE simple play, run it
  • Praise effort over outcome
  • Have fun — smile and laugh!

🌟 Coach's Reminder

"You're not coaching football. You're coaching confidence, teamwork, and joy. The X's and O's are just the vehicle." 🏈❤️
